## PROJECT XANADU: Your Complete Vision

Xanadu is not (as often supposed) an attempt to create the World Wide Web; it is a sweeping design for text, audio and video as an endless fabric of strips and streams visibly connected by links and identities of content. This minimal design intrinsically solves a dozen problems.

### The Fundamental Design Principles

**1. The True Structure of Information**
- "What is the true structure? Having answered that, you design a system that stores and shows that true structure."
- The structure a user sees should be the intrinsic structure of his material, not some amalgam combining the material itself with some set of obtrusive conventions under which it is stored.
- What the thing is--its natural structure to the user--is what he should see and work on: nothing less, nothing more, nothing else.

**2. Prismatic Storage (Versioning)**
- Text is best viewed as an evolving, Protean structure
- The true storage of text should be in a system that stores each change and fragment individually, assimilating each change as it arrives, but keeping the former changes
- Think of the process of making editorial changes as re-twisting a braid when its parts are rearranged, added or subtracted
- Successive versions of the document, at successive instants of time, are slices in this space-time vortex
- The user may refer not merely to the present version of the document; he or she may go back in time to any previous version
- "You get the part you want; the mistake of the conventional computer field has been to assume that the whole document had to be formed and ready."

**3. Transclusion (Not Copying)**
- The same content appearing in multiple places, not copied but linked
- Through a "window" in the new document we see a portion of the old
- No copy is made of the quoted material; rather, a quote-link symbol is placed in the stored symbol-train of the quoting document
- This quotation does not affect the integrity or uniqueness of the original document, since no copy is made
- "Since the copyright holder gets an automatic royalty, anything may be quoted without further permission."

**4. Two-Way Links**
- Links that work in both directions, showing what links to what
- The reader should be able to ask, for a given document or place in the document, "What connects here from other documents?"
- Links stay with specific characters, wherever they go through versions
- A link to one version of a Prismatic Document is a link to all versions

**5. The Document Convention**
- Everything stored is divided precisely into separate documents
- A document consists of anything that someone wishes to store
- Every document has an owner
- A document consists of its contents and its out-links
- Links may be created within or between documents, but each link resides in one place

**6. Compound Documents (Windowing)**
- A document may have a window to another document, and that one to yet another, indefinitely
- Think of the present document as a sheet of glass. It may have writing painted on it by the present author; it may have windows to something else, in turn made of more layers of painted glass, with writing on each
- A reader may either explore the immediate document, or step through the windows to explore the next document, or the one beyond

**7. Automatic Micropayments (Royalties)**
- A standard proportional fee is paid automatically by the user to the owner every time a fragment is summoned
- Say, a few cents per screen hour--and each reader contributes those few cents automatically as he reads along
- "You publish something, anyone can quote it, you always get a royalty automatically. Fair."
- If a modified document is read, the original owner and the modifier split the royalty in proportion to who wrote what, as determined automatically

**8. The Docuverse**
- All documents and their links unite into what is essentially a swirling complex of equi-accessible unity, a single great universal text and data grid
- Everything on such a network can come immediately, even as it grows to tremendous size
- "We mean very quickly, even though there will be variations. If you telephone San Francisco from Chicago, you get through 'immediately'-- that is, within perhaps three to fifteen seconds."

**9. No Withdrawal, Only Supersession**
- Publishing is a solemn act, and it is in the common interest that a thing once published stay published
- The author may not withdraw it except by lengthy due process
- However, the author may readily publish a superseding document, but the former version remains on the network
- This is vital because of the links other users may have made to it

**10. Sieving (Filtering In-Links)**
- The reader should be able to ask "What connects here from other documents?" and be shown all these outside connections
- But there may be too many--for "Alice in Wonderland" or the U.S. Constitution, the number could be in the squillions
- Thus it becomes necessary to apply some kind of filter: "What links come in from Spain? From last week? From last year in Marienbad?"
- This filtering by different attributes we call "sieving"

### Why Xanadu is Simpler Than the Web

- Xanadu is far simpler than the Web, but utterly incompatible because of the Web's iron browser standard, which forbids all Xanadu methods and views
- "We fight on," say Xanadu diehards
- The design is simple and basic, like the telephone
- "We regard the simplicity of this design as its greatest virtue. The user has no direct contact with technicalities."

## Your Key Criticisms

### Hierarchy is the Official Metaphysic

- "Hierarchy is the official metaphysic of the computer world. (Aristotle, the medieval Catholic Church and the Dewey Decimal System have all reinforced this concept.) Many tekkies think all structure is hierarchical, and have arranged not to see any other kinds."
- "They say if you have a hammer everything looks like a nail. Today's hierarchical computer tools (especially object-oriented languages and XML) make hierarchy an imposition, not an option."
- "Current tools cannot represent cross-connection, interpenetration, overlap, or the other tangles of the real world-- let alone opinions about it."
- "The Harvard outline, in the 20th Century, has had a powerful influence; people are told that organizing hierarchically is 'thinking logically', though it means cutting many connections and associations to select a few."

### The Computer Priesthood

- "Knowledge is power and so it tends to be hoarded. Experts in any field rarely want people to understand what they do, and generally enjoy putting people down."
- "If we say that the use of computers is dominated by a priesthood, people who spatter you with unintelligible answers and seem unwilling to give you straight ones, it is not that they are different in this respect from any other profession."
- "But computers are very special, and we have to deal with them everywhere, and this effectively gives the computer priesthood a stranglehold on the operation of all large organizations."
- "It is imperative for many reasons that the appalling gap between public and computer insider be closed. As the saying goes, war is too important to be left to the generals. Guardianship of the computer can no longer be left to a priesthood."

### What Literature Really Is

- "Literature is an ongoing system of interconnecting documents."
- "A literature is a system of interconnected writings. We do not offer this as our definition, but as a discovered fact. And almost all writing is part of some literature."
- "The way people write is based in large part on these interconnections."
- "Within bodies of writing, everywhere, there are linkages we tend not to see. The individual document, at hand, is what we deal with; we do not see the total linked collection of them all at once. But they are there, the documents not present as well as those that are, and the grand cat's-cradle among them all."

### The Web's Fundamental Flaws

- One-way links that break constantly
- No versioning--we can't see how documents change over time
- No transclusion--content is copied, not linked, leading to duplication and inconsistency
- Hierarchical structure imposed everywhere
- No automatic micropayments
- No two-way links
- "The web is a joke compared to what I envisioned"

## Key Xanadu Technical Concepts (For Reference)

### Prismatic Storage
- Stores fragments, not whole documents
- Tracks all changes chronologically
- Reconstructs any version instantly ("pounce")
- Alternative versions share common storage

### Transclusion (Windowing)
- Quote-links, not copies
- Windows to other documents
- Original remains unchanged
- Automatic royalty distribution

### Two-Way Links
- Links attach to specific characters, not positions
- Links survive version changes
- Can query "what links here?"
- Sieving filters in-links by attributes

### Document Structure
- Every document has an owner
- Documents consist of contents and out-links
- Links are part of documents
- Compound documents window to others

### Micropayments
- Automatic proportional royalties
- Based on transmission time
- Split between original and modifier
- Standard rates, fair distribution

### The Docuverse
- Single unified accessible whole
- Distributed but appears unified
- Instant retrieval ("immediately")
- Grows without limit
